About Me

I am an EPSRC postdoctoral fellow at the Mathematical Institute, University of Warwick, hosted by Prof. Martin Lotz. Previously, I was a postdoctoral fellow at the University of Colorado at Boulder working under the supervision of Prof. Joshua Grochow. I obtained a PhD in May 2020 from Purdue University supervised by Prof. Saugata Basu and Prof. Elena Grigorescu.

At a very high level, I would describe my research interests as algebraic methods (broadly interpreted), taking inspiration from mathematical applications. Some specific interests are in (topological questions in) real-algebraic geometry, o-minimal geometry, and their applications in, for example, combinatorics and computational complexity theory.

Earlier, I got a masters degree from Brown University, USA, and a bachelors degree from R. V. College of Engineering, Bangalore, India.

I organized the conference OMIGAWD: O-Minimal Geometry - Interactions, Applications and Wider Developments, 2025, with support from my EPSRC grant and a grant from the University of Warwick's MRC.
